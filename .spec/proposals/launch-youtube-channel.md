# Launch YouTube Channel

## Binding Problem

Source video: https://youtu.be/qtuikW9wvhY

The teaching work (WS7) needs a distribution channel beyond the published HTML site. YouTube is the natural fit — but the standard YouTube playbook (consistent schedule, algorithm-chasing, thumbnail engineering) is the wrong shape for what this is. The challenge is launching a channel that's honest about its cadence and doesn't generate the wrong kind of pressure.

## Success Criteria

- Channel exists with a stated philosophy ("real, irregular, no schedule promises").
- First 3 videos shipped, drawing from existing study/ or yt/ content.
- A repeatable production pipeline (probably involves chip-voice + ai-presentation-site-tool + obs).
- Explicit non-goals: no daily uploads, no shorts farm, no thumbnail psyops.

## Constraints

- Must be honest. The channel is the teaching, not a brand.
- Production pipeline must be agent-assisted or it won't sustain.

## Related

- Depends on `ai-presentation-site-tool` for visual generation.
- Pairs with chip-voice for narration.
- Probably blocked on having a clear "first 3 videos" picked from existing studies.

## Phase 1

Watch the source video. Pick the first 3 study topics that translate well. Decide if the first video is a "channel intro / philosophy" or a content drop.

## Costs / Risks

- Real time investment per video.
- Risk of treadmill creep — guard against it explicitly.
